# Curatorial Review

## Exhibition Concept

The goal of RATER is to reinterpret everyday rating systems as cultural artifacts.  
Rather than focusing on the practical use of ratings, the site highlights the deeper social structures behind them.

The exhibition frames evaluation as a cultural ritual. Every rating system attempts to convert subjective taste into objective numbers, but these systems inevitably reflect the values of the societies that create them.

By presenting these mechanisms as museum artifacts, the project invites visitors to reflect on the ways they judge quality, status, and legitimacy.

## Narrative Structure

The site follows a museum-style progression.

Visitors first encounter the conceptual thesis in the introduction.  
They are then guided through a collection of conceptual artifacts that represent different forms of evaluation.

The final sections reflect on the broader implications of ranking systems in digital culture.

## Design Decisions

Several design principles guided the visual structure of the exhibition:

### Spatial pacing
Large areas of whitespace create a slower reading experience, similar to walking through a gallery.

### Typography hierarchy
Large headings and restrained text blocks guide the visitor through the narrative structure.

### Numerical symbolism
Circular score elements represent the cultural power of numbers and ratings.

### Motion and interaction
Subtle scroll animations reveal sections gradually, encouraging visitors to progress through the exhibition sequentially.

## Critical Reflection

While rating systems attempt to standardize judgment, they also reinforce cultural hierarchies and social signaling.

RATER presents these systems not as neutral tools but as expressions of collective perception.

The exhibition ultimately asks a simple question:

If numbers claim objectivity, what do they reveal about the people who create them?
