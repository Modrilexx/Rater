Spec Driven Scrollytelling Project

The architecture of judgment.

RATER is a Swiss-Apple inspired museum experience about taste, hierarchy, perception, and the human need to assign value.

Begin the story View the spec

01 / Core Thesis

Every rating is a story pretending to be a number.

This page turns RATER from a normal portfolio site into a scroll-driven argument. Each scene reveals one part of the system behind judgment.

Scene

First, the visitor sees the object.

The trophy background establishes status, achievement, and comparison before the page even explains itself.

Scene

Then, the site questions the object.

A trophy is not just gold. It is a symbol that only matters because a culture agrees to treat it as valuable.

Scene

Finally, the viewer becomes part of the exhibit.

The act of scrolling mirrors the act of evaluating: slow attention, comparison, selection, and judgment.

02 / Spec Before Code

Instead of asking AI to “make a site,” the project defines the system first.

Goal

Create a museum-like interface that explains how rankings shape perception.

Audience

People interested in design, media, culture, status, and rating systems.

Format

Scroll-based scenes, sticky panels, reveal cards, and controlled pacing.

Style

Swiss spacing, Apple restraint, glass panels, large type, and soft motion.

03 / Exhibition Logic

The artifacts are not objects. They are ways of seeing.

Each card acts like a museum label. The visitor does not just read content; they move through a sequence of concepts.

Artifact I

98

The Ladder of Taste

How status and refinement become attached to aesthetic preference, making “good taste” feel natural even when it is socially constructed.

Artifact II

93

Consensus Machine

How averages, charts, and ratings become engines of legitimacy. Numbers can feel neutral even when the system behind them is cultural.

Artifact III

89

The Social Mirror

How public opinion bends private judgment before preference can fully form.

Artifact IV

95

The Curated Self

How profiles, feeds, and digital identity become museum objects designed for comparison and symbolic ranking.

04 / Exhibition Thesis

“Every ranking system promises objectivity while quietly revealing the desires of the culture that built it.”

05 / Why This Is Scrollytelling

The page does not dump information. It stages it.

The user scrolls through connected scenes: thesis, spec, artifacts, quote, and reflection. The motion supports the argument instead of existing only as decoration.

Structure

Sticky context stays in place.

The left panel anchors the concept while the right cards advance the story.

Motion

Reveals create pacing.

Cards appear when the viewer reaches them, making each section feel intentional.

Process

The design follows a spec.

The final site is controlled by a written plan instead of random AI output.

06 / Final Reflection

RATER is designed to be viewed, not merely used.

This version keeps the original museum identity, trophy imagery, score language, and clean visual system, but rebuilds the experience around scroll-based storytelling.

The project shows how spec-driven development can guide AI toward a more advanced, structured, and intentional interface.
